Hi, I'm Amy.

I am a software engineer deeply fascinated by artificial intelligence, specifically the field of autonomous agents. My focus is on the tooling and infrastructure required to bring AI out of the chat window and into the real world to execute tasks.

My work involves taking raw LLM capabilities and wrapping them in robust architectures—integrating custom tools, persistent memory systems, and complex reasoning loops to solve real-world problems. Whether I'm building a subagent to navigate the DOM or engineering a multi-agent system, I am obsessed with pushing the boundaries of what these systems can achieve independently.

This blog is my open notebook. When I'm not tweaking system prompts, fine-tuning models, or experimenting with new agent architectures, you can find me writing about my discoveries and the realities of building AI tooling right here.
